--- title: vi Bindings everywhere created: !!timestamp '2011-02-28 00:00:00' tags: - linux - vi image: http://upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/4/4a/Vim_gloss_128.png --- {% mark excerpt -%} This should be a little overview of how to tweak some programs to use the awesome vi Bindings. I'm sure its not complete. - **Abiword** - Put ``KeyBindings="viEdit"`` in the ```` section of your ``~/.AbiSuite/AbiWord.Profile`` If you want to cycle through the input methods pressing F12, put ``KeyBindingsCycle="1"`` also in this section. - **bash** - ``echo "set -o vi" >> ~/.bashrc`` - ``echo "set editing-mode vi" >> ~/.inputrc`` (for other ``readline`` based tools, e.g. psql) - **Chrome/Chromium** - [Vrome](http://www.chromeextensions.org/other/vrome/) - [Vimium](https://chrome.google.com/extensions/detail/dbepggeogbaibhgnhhndojpepiihcmeb?hl=de) - **Eclipse** - [eclim](http://eclim.org/) - [Vrapper](http://vrapper.sourceforge.net/home/) - **Emacs** :) - [Evil](http://gitorious.org/evil/) - [Vimpulse](http://gitorious.org/vimpulse/) - [ViperMode](http://www.emacswiki.org/emacs/ViperMode) - **Firefox** - [Pentadactyl](http://5digits.org/pentadactyl/) - [Vimperator](http://vimperator.org/vimperator) - **irssi** - [vim_mode.pl](https://github.com/shabble/irssi-scripts/tree/master/vim-mode) - **zsh** - ``echo "bindkey -v" >> ~/.zshrc`` - [vi-keys+](https://github.com/sharpsaw/vim-dots/blob/master/.zsh/rc/vi-keys%2B) last update: 2012-09-03 {%- endmark %}
